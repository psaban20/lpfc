WHO WE ARE

Building LaPorte's Soccer Future, One Player at a Time

At the heart of the Club are the hardworking and resilient people of LaPorte.

LaPorte Futbol Club was founded in November 2021 when fourteen community members came together with a vision: to reorganize and revitalize youth soccer in LaPorte. We saw kids with untapped potential, families seeking quality athletic opportunities, and a community that deserved better.

In just three years, we've grown from an idea into a thriving organization serving nearly 400 young athletes and their families. We are a 100% volunteer-run 501(c)(3) non-profit, governed by a dedicated board of directors who believe deeply in soccer's power to transform lives.

Our Philosophy

We're in the business of personal development—soccer just happens to be our context. Through the game, we teach discipline, teamwork, resilience, and leadership. We help kids learn to take risks, handle success and failure, and push through discomfort.

OUR IMPACT & GROWTH

Proof That LaPorte Wants This

Three Years of Explosive Growth

When we reorganized in 2021, we started with 293 kids from 219 families. In 2023, we grew to 308 kids from 239 families. But 2024 has been our breakthrough year: we now serve 387 kids from 299 families, representing 25% growth in players and 20% growth in families.

This isn't just about numbers. It's about momentum. It's about families telling other families: "You should try LaPorte FC." It's about kids coming home excited about practice. It's about the community responding to quality.

Community Reach

  • 36,000 Facebook views (20% growth year-over-year)
  • 1,033 social media followers
  • Reach across LaPorte and surrounding communities including Michigan City, Westville, Rolling Prairie, and beyond
  • Primary audience: families aged 25-44 (parents of young children)

Health Impact

On average, a LaPorte FC player receives 3 hours of moderate to vigorous physical activity per week. In an era of childhood obesity and screen addiction, we're giving kids the gift of movement, fresh air, and physical challenge. We're building healthy habits that will last a lifetime.

THE ACADEMY

Reversing Pay-to-Play - Quality Soccer, Accessible to All

The Problem with American Youth Soccer

In the United States, youth soccer operates on a "pay-to-play" model. Families pay hundreds or thousands of dollars per year for their children to participate. Higher levels of competition cost more. Elite training costs even more.

The result? Soccer has become a sport for families who can afford it. Talented kids from working-class families—kids with real potential—are excluded simply because they can't pay. Opportunity and potential go untapped.

Our Solution: The Academy Model

LaPorte FC is committed to implementing the Academy Model used by top soccer clubs worldwide. In this model:

  • Participation invitation-based on ability and commitment, not ability to pay
  • Talented players from all economic backgrounds identified and developed
  • Coaching, equipment, training, and competition costs subsidized
  • Quality of instruction paramount
  • Player development drives decisions, not revenue
